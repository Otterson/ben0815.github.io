Our Current Actives

Theta Tau's core values are embodied by our members who strive to develop themselves into instruments of change. They take every opportunity to build life long relationships, develop themselves into better engineers and give back to the community in any way possible. For these reasons and more it's our pleasure to introduce some of our current actives.


Kendrick Lim - Biomedical Engineering Class of 2019

Howdy! I am a senior Biomedical Engineering major from Sugar Land, Texas. I am honored to be serving as your Fall 2018 Regent of Theta Tau at Texas A&M University! As regent, I will be responsible for overseeing all functions and events of our chapter as well as helping ensure that we have a great semester. My post-graduation plans are to attend medical school and be a surgeon or emergency physician one day. I enjoy traveling, sports, doing spontaneous activities, spending time with friends, and LOSSA food!! I’m excited for what this semester will hold. Please don’t hesitate to reach out to me for anything at all!
